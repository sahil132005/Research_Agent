## Executive Summary

Quantum computing poses a significant threat to existing cryptographic systems, especially those based on public key infrastructure. Researchers are actively developing quantum-resistant algorithms to safeguard against potential quantum attacks. While quantum computers have the potential to solve complex cryptographic problems, their practical impact remains several years away. This research brief explores the role of quantum computing in cryptography, highlighting key findings, ongoing debates, and knowledge gaps.

## Topic Overview

The focus of this research is the role of quantum computing in advancing and challenging cryptographic methods. Quantum computing threatens current cryptographic algorithms while also offering opportunities for new cryptographic protocols. Researchers are working on developing quantum-resistant techniques to ensure data security in the quantum era.

## Research Findings

### Impact of Quantum Computing on Current Cryptographic Algorithms

- **Shor's algorithm** can efficiently factor large integers, undermining RSA encryption.
- Quantum computing can solve discrete logarithm problems, threatening algorithms like DSA and ECDSA.
- Symmetric cryptography is less vulnerable, though key sizes may need to be increased.

### Development of Quantum-Resistant Cryptographic Techniques

- **Lattice-based cryptography** is a promising quantum-resistant approach.
- **Hash-based cryptography** offers security against quantum attacks.
- Efforts are underway to standardize post-quantum cryptographic algorithms.

### Potential of Quantum Computing to Solve Cryptographic Problems

- Quantum computers can theoretically break widely-used public key cryptosystems.
- They may enable new cryptographic protocols based on quantum principles.
- **Quantum key distribution** provides secure communication channels resistant to eavesdropping.

### Timeline for Quantum Computing's Influence on Cryptography

- Practical quantum computers capable of breaking current cryptographic systems are still years away.
- Preparation for quantum threats is recommended within the next 10-20 years.
- Ongoing research is crucial to anticipate and mitigate future risks.

## Key Insights

- Quantum computing poses a significant threat to current cryptographic systems, particularly those based on integer factorization and discrete logarithms.
